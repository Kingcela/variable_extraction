The standard model predicts that b𝑏italic-bb quarks are produced with a large longitudinal polarization in e+⁢e-superscript𝑒superscript𝑒italic-e POSTSUPERSCRIPT:start ADDOP:plus POSTSUPERSCRIPT:end italic-e POSTSUPERSCRIPT:start ADDOP:minus POSTSUPERSCRIPT:ende^{+}e^{-} collision on the Z𝑍italic-ZZ peak[1]. The measurable b𝑏italic-bb polarization effects are reduced by the fragmentation properties of b𝑏italic-bb quarks. All spin information is lost in the formation of both pseudoscalar and vector B𝐵italic-BB mesons. Only b𝑏italic-bb-baryons are expected to retain some spin information[2].

Various methods which would be used to detect the b𝑏italic-bb polarization effects have been suggested by several authors[3, 4]. Mele and Altarelli [3] have suggested that inclusive lepton energy spectrum in the semileptonic decays of b𝑏italic-bb hadrons could be used as a measure of b𝑏italic-bb quark polarization. In this method, a good control of fragmentation effects is crucial and this may be achieved by calibration on low energy data. Also one can use the ΛbsubscriptΛ𝑏Lambda POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-b POSTSUBSCRIPT:end{\Lambda_{b}} sample of semileptonic events and compare it with the B𝐵italic-BB-meson sample in order to control fragmentation effects. In latter case, a possible observed difference between the ΛbsubscriptΛ𝑏Lambda POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-b POSTSUBSCRIPT:end{\Lambda_{b}} and the inclusive spectra could be blamed on the model dependent features of the exclusive decay and so a reliable model of ΛbsubscriptΛ𝑏Lambda POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-b POSTSUBSCRIPT:end{\Lambda_{b}} exclusive decay is required.
