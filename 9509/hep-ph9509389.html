Abstract

Starting from the symmetry of lepton flavor democracy, we propose and discuss a simple pattern for the mass generation and flavor mixing of the charged leptons and neutrinos. The three neutrino masses are nearly degenerate, and the flavor mixing angles can be calculated. In the standard model of the electroweak interactions the masses of quarks and leptons as well as the flavor mixing angles enter as free parameters. In our model for the neutrino masses the mismatch between the “democratic” mass matrix of the charged leptons and the neutrino mass matrix, where the “democratic” mass term is absent, generates large effects of flavor mixing. The associated mixing angles are large, calculable and in lowest order independent of the lepton masses. The neutrino masses consistent with the experimental constraints are nearly degenerate. One may interpret the hot dark matter component of the cosmic mass density as neutrino matter, in which the three neutrinos are expected to have masses of the order of a few eV.
