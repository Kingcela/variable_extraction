The Phase Transition of the Two Higgs extension of the Standard Model

Abstract

We present the analysis of the phase transition for the two Higgs electroweak model. We have found that for a wide range of parameters the universe first tunnels to a new intermediate phase. This feature not only is very important by itself, but also provides the essential requirements for producing baryon asymmetry with only small explicit C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP-violating terms in the two Higgs tree Lagrangian.

I INTRODUCTION

The mechanism of spontaneous symmetry breaking plays a big role in the success of the standard model of the electroweak interactions. It also leads to the possibility of phase transitions and important cosmological phenomena. This symmetry breaking mechanism is based ultimately on the scalar Higgs potential.

The current experimental situation with respect to the standard electroweak model‚Äôs Higgs sector gives the possibility for modifications. A model with two scalar boson doublets can be a sensible extension of the standard model, and may be necessary if one requires low energy supersymmetry or sufficient baryon asymmetry in the electroweak transition [3], [5].

In the two-Higgs model the desired baryogenesis at the electroweak scale has been achieved by the introduction of a term that breaks the C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP invariance explicitly at tree level. The amount of C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP violation required is uncomfortably large, particularly if the two Higgs model is the relic of a symmetry breaking at scales above the electroweak transition, when it is more natural that any explicit C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP violating terms be small [1], [2].

Here it is reported that, using the C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP invariant finite temperature effective potential, domains of the universe can first tunnel towards a new minimum before evolving to the usual minimum. When small C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP violating terms are added to the potential the C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP violation is amplified in the intermediate phase. This happens for a significant range of parameters that cover also the minimal supersymmetric model. The result is that we expect reasonably large baryon asymmetry even from a two Higgs scalar potential with very small C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP violation at low temperatures.

IV Conclusions

Kuzmin, Rubakov and Shaposhnikov [6] have argued that anomalous baryon number violation in the electroweak interactions can explain the origin of the cosmological baryon asymmetry. The problem is that in the standard model the Kobayashi-Maskawa phases give too little C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP violation to explain the observed baryon asymmetry [5]. In a two Higgs extension of the standard model, an extra source of C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP violation can come directly from a C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP non-invariant scalar potential [3], [7], [8], [4]. The amount of C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP violation necessary for the baryon asymmetry to entropy ratio infered from nucleosynthesis is difficult to reconcile with tests of the electroweak model. The present work relaxes the C‚Å¢Pùê∂ùëÉitalic-C italic-PCP requirement making more plausible the explanation of the baryogenesis coming from a realistic model.
