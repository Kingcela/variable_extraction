I. INTRODUCTION:

Due to their unusual properties caused by highly degenerate ground states, antiferromagnetic Potts models have been attracting much attention. Some recent studies focussed particularly on the three-state antiferromagnetic Potts model on a simple cubic lattice. Field theoretical calculations, renormalization group theory, Monte Carlo simulations, the cluster-variation method[1], and the coherent-anomaly method[2] have been applied to obtain information about its critical behavior. Reference 3 gives a detailed history of developments.

Qualitatively our results could be explained in the following way: If one looks at the PSS state as a configuration with, i.e., state 1 dominating sublattice aùëéitalic-aa and state 2 dominating sublattice bùëèitalic-bb, one can start to ‚Äúrain down‚Äù randomly state 3 spins onto the whole lattice. Our numerical results indicate, that this trade-off never favors the PSS over the BSS states, while RL reached a different conclusion at this point.
