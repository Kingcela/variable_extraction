Non-perturbative calculation of the probability distribution
of plane-wave transmission through a disordered waveguide

Abstract

A non-perturbative random-matrix theory is applied to the transmission of a monochromatic scalar wave through a disordered waveguide. The probability distributions of the transmittances Tm⁢nsubscript𝑇𝑚𝑛italic-T POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-m italic-n POSTSUBSCRIPT:endT_{mn} and Tn=∑mTm⁢nsubscript𝑇𝑛subscript𝑚subscript𝑇𝑚𝑛italic-T POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-n POSTSUBSCRIPT:end RELOP:equals SUMOP:sum POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-m POSTSUBSCRIPT:end italic-T POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-m italic-n POSTSUBSCRIPT:endT_{n}=\sum_{m}T_{mn} of an incident mode n𝑛italic-nn are calculated in the thick-waveguide limit, for broken time-reversal symmetry. A qualitatively different crossover occurs if the disordered region is replaced by a chaotic cavity.

In conclusion, we have presented a non-perturbative calculation of the distributions of the plane-wave transmittances Tm⁢nsubscript𝑇𝑚𝑛italic-T POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-m italic-n POSTSUBSCRIPT:endT_{mn} and Tnsubscript𝑇𝑛italic-T POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-n POSTSUBSCRIPT:endT_{n} through a disordered waveguide without time-reversal symmetry, which shows how the distributions cross over from Rayleigh and Gaussian statistics in the diffusive regime, to a common lognormal distribution in the localized regime.
